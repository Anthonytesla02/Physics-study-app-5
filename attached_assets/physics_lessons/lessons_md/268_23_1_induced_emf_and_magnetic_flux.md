## 23.1 Induced Emf and Magnetic Flux

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:

*   Calculate the flux of a uniform magnetic field through a loop of arbitrary orientation.
*   Describe methods to produce an electromotive force (emf) with a magnetic field or magnet and a loop of wire.

The apparatus used by Faraday to demonstrate that magnetic fields can create currents is illustrated in [Figure 23.3](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169738208118). When the switch is closed, a magnetic field is produced in the coil on the top part of the iron ring and transmitted to the coil on the bottom part of the ring. The galvanometer is used to detect any current induced in the coil on the bottom. It was found that each time the switch is closed, the galvanometer detects a current in one direction in the coil on the bottom. (You can also observe this in a physics lab.) Each time the switch is opened, the galvanometer detects a current in the opposite direction. Interestingly, if the switch remains closed or open for any length of time, there is no current through the galvanometer. _Closing and opening the switch_ induces the current. It is the _change_ in magnetic field that creates the current. More basic than the current that flows is the _emf_ that causes it. The current is a result of an _emf induced by a changing magnetic field_, whether or not there is a path for current to flow.

An experiment easily performed and often done in physics labs is illustrated in [Figure 23.4](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169738076827). An emf is induced in the coil when a bar magnet is pushed in and out of it. Emfs of opposite signs are produced by motion in opposite directions, and the emfs are also reversed by reversing poles. The same results are produced if the coil is moved rather than the magnet—it is the relative motion that is important. The faster the motion, the greater the emf, and there is no emf when the magnet is stationary relative to the coil.

The method of inducing an emf used in most electric generators is shown in [Figure 23.5](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169737818705). A coil is rotated in a magnetic field, producing an alternating current emf, which depends on rotation rate and other factors that will be explored in later sections. Note that the generator is remarkably similar in construction to a motor (another symmetry).

So we see that changing the magnitude or direction of a magnetic field produces an emf. Experiments revealed that there is a crucial quantity called the magnetic flux, ΦΦ , given by

Φ\=BAcosθ,Φ\=BAcosθ,

where _BB_ is the magnetic field strength over an area _AA_, at an angle θ θ with the perpendicular to the area as shown in [Figure 23.6](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169737961842). **Any change in magnetic flux ΦΦ induces an emf.** This process is defined to be electromagnetic induction. Units of magnetic flux _ΦΦ_ are T⋅m2T⋅m2. As seen in [Figure 23.6](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169737961842), Bcosθ\=BBcosθ\=B⊥, which is the component of _BB_ perpendicular to the area _AA_. Thus magnetic flux is Φ\=B⊥AΦ\=B⊥A, the product of the area and the component of the magnetic field perpendicular to it.

Figure 23.6 Magnetic flux ΦΦ is related to the magnetic field and the area over which it exists. The flux Φ\=BAcosθΦ\=BAcosθ is related to induction; any change in ΦΦ induces an emf.

All induction, including the examples given so far, arises from some change in magnetic flux _ΦΦ_. For example, Faraday changed _BB_ and hence _ΦΦ_ when opening and closing the switch in his apparatus (shown in [Figure 23.3](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169738208118)). This is also true for the bar magnet and coil shown in [Figure 23.4](23-1-induced-emf-and-magnetic-flux#import-auto-id1169738076827). When rotating the coil of a generator, the angle θθ and, hence, _ΦΦ_ is changed. Just how great an emf and what direction it takes depend on the change in _ΦΦ_ and how rapidly the change is made, as examined in the next section.
